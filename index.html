Hackathon Q&A

Q:
Do I need to build a complete, fully functional product by the end of the hackathon?
A:

Given the time constraints of the hackathon, we do not expect participants to produce a fully coded and functional product by the end of the event. Having a strong idea and putting together a solid prototype or project skeleton is already enough.

Q:
Would I mainly be expected to identify legal use cases or fintech applications for Ripple?
A:

Not necessarily. As long as it relates to social good in a broad sense, it would be within scope. The specific direction is largely up to your team and the kind of issue you want to address.

Q:
What if I have limited experience in coding?
A:

This event brings together students from both the School of Law and the Computer Science, teams will have representatives from both schools. The interdisciplinary nature of the event is specifically meant to allow people with different strengths to support one another, regardless of coding background.

Participants are also welcome to use Claude in ways that are permitted under the rules of the School of Law.

Q:
Will there be technical support during the event?
A:

Yes. Ripple engineers and CS professors will be there to help.
